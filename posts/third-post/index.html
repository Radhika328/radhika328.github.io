Images as Information

February 18, 2026

In this unit, I learned that images can also be seen with a perspective of data and math. I learned how to transform and optimize images by seeing them as changeable information. For me photos were stuff which I can store them as memory, but this unit changed my perspective. I started taking images in a more technical way, and I started describing them using math and data.

This unit made me realize that the image is made up of thousands of tiny units called pixels. This idea made me connect the topic to things I already use, like photo editing apps. I never thought about how changing brightness or color actually works, but now I understand that it is happening by changing pixel values. I also related the idea of colors to what I had learned earlier in painting, where colors mix to form new ones. This connection made it easier for me to understand.

However, I did find some parts difficult. For me, to understand how using numbers and simple functions can change the image, was a bit difficult. The idea of optimizing images was also confusing for me because I had never learned anything like that before. I always made mistakes while working with pixel values because my predicted answer was wrong many times. 

Discussing and learning together helped me to understand the concepts better. With the help of  group members it is easy to clarify doubts also. This unit taught me that even simple things like images can be understood in a different way, and learning becomes easier when we help each other.
