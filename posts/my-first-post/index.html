**DIKW MODEL**

February 4, 2026

In the first unit of the course, I learned about the DIKW model, which stands for Data, Information, Knowledge, and Wisdom. Before taking this class, I had heard the words data and information many times, but I never clearly understood the difference between them. Through this unit, I learned that data refers to raw facts or numbers that do not have meaning by themselves. When this data is organized or given context, it becomes information.At first, the concept was new to me because I usually thought that data and information meant the same thing. However, during the class discussion, I realized that data is just raw numbers or facts without meaning. When data is organized or processed, it becomes information.With that information we learn something then it becomes knowledge . Finally, wisdom is when we use that knowledge to make good decisions.One thing I found interesting was how this model connects to everyday life. For example, a list of temperatures is data. When we organize it to see weather patterns, it becomes information. Seeing the pattern gives knowledge, and taking an umbrella because of it shows wisdom. This example helped me understand the idea more clearly. At first, I found it a little hard to remember the four levels, but drawing a pyramid and thinking about easy real-life examples made it easier. Overall, learning the DIKW model helped me see how simple data can turn into information and then knowledge. It showed me how basic facts can be useful in everyday life.
